CANCELLED! 2020 CSSS meeting, Charlottetown, PE, June 21-25th

BUILDING BRIDGES AND CONNECTING SOIL PROPERTIES

CSSS 2020 has been cancelled due to COVID-19. We hope to see you in Charlottetown in 2021!

Dear CSSS participants,

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the 2020 meeting of the CSSS scheduled for June 21–25 in PEI is cancelled. The CSSS council met with members of the PEI local organizing committee and representatives from the 2021 (Scott Chang) and 2022 (David Burton) organizing committees to discuss options. Both Scott and David's teams graciously agreed to push their planned meetings back by 1 year. Choosing this option means that many of the efforts and expenses already made for the 2020 meeting will not be lost, but rather deferred to 2021. We are also grateful that the PEI organizing committee (led by Judith Nyrianeza and Scott Anderson) is committed to organize/host the meeting in 2021. Stay tuned for specific details.

A few other related points: 1) Unless a refund is requested, registrations will automatically be deferred to the 2021 meeting. More details will follow. 2) Accordingly, there will be no 2020 Student Travel Awards to attend our society meeting (nor will there be student presentation awards); however nominations/applications for all other awards (e.g. CSSS Fellow) will still be accepted https://csss.ca/awards-criteria/ 3) The 2020 CSSS AGM, which would have been held during the PEI meetings, will now be held virtually (e.g. via a Zoom meeting with phone-in options). The date/time/details will follow shortly after the next CSSS Council meeting in April.

Thanks to everyone for their patience, understanding, and ongoing commitment to the CSSS, and again a huge thanks to the work and flexibility from the meeting organizers. On behalf of the CSSS Council, we wish everyone well during this trying and unprecedented time.

We are excited to host the 2021 annual meeting of the Canadian Society of Soil Science in Charlottetown. The organizing committee will welcome you at the birthplace of confederation where agriculture, tourism and fishing represent the largest industries. The meeting will start with interesting pre-conference workshops (details to follow), followed by three full days of sessions and the fourth day will be dedicated to field trips (details to follow). The theme of the meeting is “Building bridges and connecting soil processes”. The keynote speakers and scientific sessions will cover diverse and interesting topics including: soil ecology, impact of agricultural practices on soil health, linking soil organisms to ecosystems services, soil nutrient management and water quality, greenhouse gas measurements and modelling, precision agriculture and big data management, along with methods of soil mapping.

We look forward to seeing you in Charlottetown. The island will charm you with its beautiful beaches, red sands and cliffs, and more importantly with its cuisine characterized by fresh and various local ingredients. The meeting will correspond with the beginning of lobster season.
